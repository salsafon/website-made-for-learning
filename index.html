Introduction to Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for over 160 million years, from the Triassic period (about 230 million years ago) to the end of the Cretaceous period (about 66 million years ago). They evolved into a wide variety of species, ranging from small, bird-like creatures to massive, towering predators.

Explore Dinosaur Types

Carnivores

Carnivorous dinosaurs were meat-eating predators with sharp teeth, strong jaws, and powerful limbs. Most were theropods, walking on two legs and hunting prey.

Omnivores

Omnivorous dinosaurs ate both plants and animals, with adaptable diets including fruits, insects, and eggs. Many were theropods, using beaks or teeth to forage and hunt.

Herbivores

Herbivorous dinosaurs ate plants, using specialized teeth and jaws to chew leaves, ferns, and fruits. Many were large, walking on four legs.
